The Birth of Everything

Around 13.8 billion years ago, there was no space, no time, no matter—only potential. Then came the Big Bang. Not an explosion in space, but an expansion of space itself. In a fraction of a second, the Universe expanded faster than the speed of light. Energy cooled into particles. Particles formed atoms. Atoms gathered into clouds. From chaos came structure. From simplicity came complexity.
