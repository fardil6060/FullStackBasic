Galaxies – Cities of Stars

Stars rarely exist alone. They gather into vast systems called galaxies. There are hundreds of billions of galaxies, each containing billions or even trillions of stars. Types of Galaxies • Spiral galaxies – graceful arms spinning around a core (like the Milky Way) • Elliptical galaxies – massive, smooth, ancient • Irregular galaxies – chaotic, shaped by collisions At the center of almost every large galaxy lies a supermassive black hole, millions to billions of times the mass of our Sun. These monsters don’t destroy galaxies—they help shape them.
